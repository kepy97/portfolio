ABB Internship Interview Experience

Finally, Got an Internship in Fortune 500 Company!!

As an international student, it is very hard to secure an internship in the USA. In fact, international student even can't get phone interview so getting a video or in-person interview is a big achievement. In this post, I have written my whole internship hunting experience.

I'm a graduate student at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee. I enrolled in Fall-2019. In the starting of November 2019, I started to apply for the internship application. Big companies already started to hire interns. I show many student posts about they got the offer for the Summer 2020 internship. I was feeling nervous because I'm getting only online coding challenges. It was a bad time for me.

Created Personal Website

I started wondering about what is wrong in my application. And I figured out it's because I'm on an F-1 visa. Companies are looking for long-term players only and they have to pay extra money for sponsorship. That's the main problem.

I need to do something different than other applications so I thought I should create my portfolio. Many people suggest that resume must be only 1 page. But, I can only put a few information in my resume so a personal website is a good idea to include more project information. So, I created a static website before January 2020.

University Career & Internship Fair

I attended a career fair at my university in the end of February 2020. However, I have applied to more than 200 companies for software development positions before the career fair.

After attending a career fair, I was feeling it is worthless because many companies representative doesn't know anything about the position. They were giving a business card or page and ask me to apply through online by a mentioned website. They were not even interested to take a look at the resume. Many companies do not hire international students, they were looking for US citizens. But, I decided to go to every companies table and leave my resume. If they don't ask for my resume, I insisted them to keep my resume.

Luck?

I was getting many rejection emails and few of them are instant emails after submitting the application. I think that's an auto-generated email where if applicant select as not US citizen, he/she will get an email. One week passed after attending a career fair. Nothing was working out. So, I even stopped to apply for new companies because of frustration.

However, I got some good emails among them. I responded to those emails and scheduled an in-person interview with 2 companies. One of them was ABB(I got an email from ABB on Friday, March 6th). I went to the ABB site in Wisconsin office on Tuesday, March 10th for interview.

The interview went well. I was confident that I will get this job. After 2 days(March 12th), I got a congratulation email from my manager. I was so happy on that day. I like to thanks my manger for trusting me and giving me a chance. Happy ending :)

How did they got my resume?

I did not mention about how I got an in-person interview. As I said earlier, I went to every company table in my university career fair. After one week, ABB's manager emailed me that he got a resume from the HR department and he was looking for an intern to complete one project in the summer. So, the university career fair helped me to get a summer internship in my case even I was thinking career fair is worthless.

Interviewing is a lot about luck, trust me, but the only thing in your hand is practice and perseverance.

I know that was a long post but this is it, my full experience and journey into getting an offer from ABB. I was lucky but prepared. Goodluck with your journey!
